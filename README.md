# HR Attrition Power BI Report

A simple Power BI dashboard that shows why employees leave the company and how to keep them.

## 📊 What This Report Does

This report looks at employee data to find out which employees are likely to leave and why. It helps HR teams understand the main reasons people quit and what can be done to stop it.

## 📈 Main Dashboard 1: Attrition Overview

This dashboard shows detailed information about employee departures.

**Main Numbers:**
- 16% of employees left the company
- 1,470 total employees in the company
- 237 employees quit this year
- Average job satisfaction: 2.72 out of 4 (quite low)

**What the Charts Show:**
- **Job Satisfaction Impact**: Employees who are unhappy with their job leave more often
- **Problem Job Roles**: Some types of jobs have much higher quit rates than others
- **Department Concerns**: Sales, HR, and Research teams have different exit rates
- **Travel Effects**: Employees who travel a lot are more likely to leave
- **Age Groups**: Younger and older employees leave at different rates
- **Job Details Table**: Shows which jobs are riskier based on age, satisfaction, and overtime

##  Main Dashboard 2: Company Overview & Salary Analysis

This dashboard shows general company information and salary trends.

**Main Company Numbers:**
- Average employee age: 37 years old
- Total employees: 1,470
- Average satisfaction level: 2.72 out of 4
- Average salary increase: 15%
- More men than women in the company

**What the Charts Show:**
- **Employees by Department**: How many people work in each department
- **Employees by Age**: How many people are in each age group
- **Salary Growth Chart**: Shows how salary increases as people get older
- **Job Details by Role**: Specific data for each job type with satisfaction, overtime, and tenure information

## 🔍 Key Insights

**Jobs with Most Quitting Employees:**
- **Research Scientists**: 33% quit rate (BIGGEST PROBLEM)
- **Jobs with lots of overtime**: People quit more when they work too much
- **Low satisfaction jobs**: Unhappy employees leave the company

**Different Departments, Different Problems:**
- **Sales**: Has the most employees but losing some
- **Research & Development**: Losing more employees than other departments
- **Human Resources**: Losing very few employees

**Age Group Patterns:**
- Younger people (18-24 years old) leave more often
- Older employees (45+) tend to stay longer
- Most employees have been here for 11 years on average

##  What The Company Should Do

**Stop People From Quitting:**

1. **Make People Happier at Work**: Ask employees what makes them unhappy and fix it. Happy employees don't leave.

2. **Stop Forcing Overtime**: Let people go home on time. Working too much makes people tired and they quit. Especially fix this for Research Scientists and similar roles.

3. **Give Better Raises**: Raise salaries for jobs where people quit the most. Right now average raise is only 15%.

4. **Reduce Travel Requirements**: Some jobs make people travel too much. Let them stay home more - it helps them stay with the company.

5. **Talk to Research Teams**: They quit more than other departments. Find out what's wrong and fix their working conditions.

6. **Help Young Employees**: Younger people (18-35 years old) leave more often. Give them career growth chances so they want to stay.

7. **Keep the Good People**: Once someone has been here 11 years, they usually stay. Use these people as mentors for new employees.
